# Quote to App

## Overview
Transform quotes and wisdom from personalities people admire into interactive web applications that help users embody those principles and thinking patterns.

## Problem Statement
People are inspired by quotes and figures they admire but struggle to consistently think and act like them. Quotes provide momentary inspiration but not sustained behavioral change.

## Target Audience
- People with role models and mentors
- Personal development enthusiasts
- Professionals modeling successful leaders
- Students learning from historical figures
- Anyone seeking to embody specific values

## Core Features
- Personality-based web apps (Stoics, entrepreneurs, scientists, etc.)
- Quote database for each personality
- Daily challenges based on their philosophy
- Decision-making prompts: "What would [person] do?"
- Reflection journaling in their style
- Situation analysis through their lens
- Progress tracking on embodying principles
- Examples:
  - Marcus Aurelius: stoic practice app
  - Naval Ravikant: decision-making framework
  - Marie Curie: scientific thinking exercises
  - Fred Rogers: kindness and patience practice

## Value Proposition
Move from inspiration to imitation - let your heroes guide your daily decisions and personal growth through structured practice.

## Technical Approach
- Quote and philosophy database per personality
- LLM fine-tuned on person's writing/thinking style
- Daily prompt and challenge system
- Decision support tool ("Ask [personality]")
- Journaling and reflection interface
- Progress and insight tracking

## Testing Business Ideas

### Critical Assumptions to Test

#### Desirability Assumptions
1. **People want to embody specific personalities**
   - Risk: High | Evidence: Low

2. **Daily prompts more valuable than quote consumption**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: Low

3. **Users will engage with AI personality daily**
   - Risk: High | Evidence: Low

4. **Willing to pay $5-10/month for this**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: Low

#### Feasibility Assumptions
5. **AI can simulate personality thinking patterns**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: Medium

6. **Can curate enough content per personality**
   - Risk: Low | Evidence: High

7. **Accuracy concerns can be addressed**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: Medium

#### Viability Assumptions
8. **Can differentiate from quote apps**
   - Risk: High | Evidence: Low

9. **Can acquire users at CAC under $10**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: Low

10. **Users will stick beyond 30 days**
    - Risk: High | Evidence: Low

### Proposed Experiments

#### Experiment 1: Role Model Survey
- **Tests:** Assumptions #1, #2
- **Method:** Survey 100 people about role models and how they apply their wisdom
- **Success Metric:** 60%+ have role models, 70%+ struggle with consistent application
- **Time/Cost:** 1 week, $100 (incentives)

#### Experiment 2: Daily Stoic Email Series
- **Tests:** Assumptions #2, #3
- **Method:** Create 30-day Marcus Aurelius email challenge, track engagement
- **Success Metric:** 30%+ open rate sustained, 20%+ complete 30 days
- **Time/Cost:** 2 weeks, $50 (email service)

#### Experiment 3: "Ask Marcus" Chatbot
- **Tests:** Assumptions #1, #5
- **Method:** Build simple chatbot trained on Meditations, test with 20 stoicism fans
- **Success Metric:** 4/5 stars, 70%+ find responses authentic and helpful
- **Time/Cost:** 3 weeks, $400

#### Experiment 4: Landing Page with Pricing
- **Tests:** Assumptions #4, #9
- **Method:** Landing page showcasing 3 personalities, collect $5 pre-orders
- **Success Metric:** 5%+ conversion, 100+ signups, CAC under $8
- **Time/Cost:** 2 weeks, $300 (ads)

#### Experiment 5: 30-Day Practice Program
- **Tests:** Assumptions #3, #10
- **Method:** Manually run Marcus Aurelius practice program for 15 people
- **Success Metric:** 60%+ complete 30 days, 70%+ report behavior change
- **Time/Cost:** 5 weeks, $0 (time investment)
