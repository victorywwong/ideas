# Book to App

## Overview
Translate self-help and educational books like "Atomic Habits" or "Peak: Science of Expertise" into interactive web applications that help users apply the concepts.

## Problem Statement
Reading books provides knowledge, but applying that knowledge consistently is hard. People need structured tools and reminders to turn book concepts into habits and actions.

## Target Audience
- Self-improvement enthusiasts
- People who buy books but struggle to apply them
- Productivity seekers
- Lifelong learners
- Coaches and mentors

## Core Features
- Book-specific interactive modules
- Daily/weekly exercises based on book frameworks
- Progress tracking and habit formation
- Personalized applications of concepts
- Reflection prompts and journaling
- Community of people applying same book
- Integration with books:
  - Atomic Habits: habit tracking and stacking
  - Peak: deliberate practice tracking
  - Deep Work: focus session management
  - Getting Things Done: task management system

## Value Proposition
Don't just read about habits and expertise - actually build them with an app designed around proven frameworks from the books you love.

## Technical Approach
- Framework extraction from books (manual curation initially)
- Web app templates per book methodology
- Habit tracking and reminder system
- Analytics and progress visualization
- Mobile-responsive design
- Potential AI coach based on book principles

## Market Analysis
### Competitors
- Generic habit trackers (not book-specific)
- Reading apps like Blinkist (summaries only)
- Course platforms (expensive, time-consuming)

### Business Model
- Per-book app purchase ($10-20)
- Subscription for multiple books ($10-15/month)
- Partnerships with publishers
- Licensing to corporate learning

## Testing Business Ideas

### Critical Assumptions to Test

#### Desirability Assumptions
1. **Book readers willing to pay for application tools**
   - Risk: High | Evidence: Low

2. **Interactive modules more effective than reading alone**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: Medium (research on active learning)

3. **Users will complete 30+ day programs**
   - Risk: High | Evidence: Low

4. **Community adds value to solo application**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: Medium

#### Feasibility Assumptions
5. **Book frameworks can be extracted reliably**
   - Risk: Low | Evidence: High (clear frameworks exist)

6. **Can secure author/publisher licensing**
   - Risk: High | Evidence: Low

7. **Single app structure works across different books**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: Medium

#### Viability Assumptions
8. **Users prefer per-book vs. subscription model**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: Low

9. **Can acquire customers at CAC under $15**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: Low

10. **Licensing costs don't exceed 30% revenue**
    - Risk: High | Evidence: Low

### Proposed Experiments

#### Experiment 1: Reader Pain Survey
- **Tests:** Assumptions #1, #2
- **Method:** Survey 100 readers of Atomic Habits about application challenges
- **Success Metric:** 70%+ struggle with application, 50%+ would pay for tools
- **Time/Cost:** 1 week, $100 (incentives)

#### Experiment 2: Atomic Habits Notion Template
- **Tests:** Assumptions #2, #3
- **Method:** Create free Atomic Habits tracker template, track usage for 30 days
- **Success Metric:** 500+ downloads, 30%+ use beyond 7 days, 10%+ complete 30 days
- **Time/Cost:** 2 weeks, $0

#### Experiment 3: Manual Coaching Concierge
- **Tests:** Assumptions #2, #3, #4
- **Method:** Manually coach 10 people through Atomic Habits framework for 30 days
- **Success Metric:** 70%+ complete program, 80%+ form target habit, 60%+ would pay
- **Time/Cost:** 5 weeks, $0 (time investment)

#### Experiment 4: Atomic Habits MVP App
- **Tests:** Assumptions #1, #7, #8
- **Method:** Build basic interactive app for Atomic Habits, charge $15, test with 50 users
- **Success Metric:** 30+ purchases, 4/5 stars, 50%+ complete program
- **Time/Cost:** 6 weeks, $1500 (development)

#### Experiment 5: Author Outreach
- **Tests:** Assumption #6
- **Method:** Contact 5 self-help authors about partnership/licensing
- **Success Metric:** 2+ positive responses, understand economics and terms
- **Time/Cost:** 2 weeks, $0
