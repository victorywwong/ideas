# Homemaid AI (Rong)

## Overview
An AI-powered bot that serves as a low-cost substitute for domestic helpers, providing household management, task scheduling, and home automation support.

## Problem Statement
Domestic helpers are expensive and not accessible to everyone. Many households need help with task management, scheduling, and home organization but can't afford traditional domestic help.

## Target Audience
- Busy professionals
- Dual-income families
- Elderly living independently
- People managing multiple responsibilities
- Budget-conscious households

## Core Features
- Task scheduling and reminders (cleaning, groceries, appointments)
- Smart home integration and automation
- Inventory management (pantry, supplies)
- Recipe suggestions based on available ingredients
- Bill tracking and payment reminders
- Voice and chat interface
- Learning family routines and preferences

## Value Proposition
Affordable AI assistant that handles household management at a fraction of the cost of hiring domestic help, available 24/7.

## Technical Approach
- LLM for natural language interaction
- Integration with smart home platforms (HomeKit, Google Home, Alexa)
- Calendar and task management APIs
- Computer vision for inventory tracking (optional)
- Personalization and routine learning
- Mobile app + voice interface

## Market Analysis
### Competitors
- Todoist, Any.do (task management only)
- Smart home assistants (limited household management)
- IFTTT/Zapier (requires technical setup)

### Business Model
- Subscription model ($10-20/month)
- Tiered pricing based on features
- Family plans

## Testing Business Ideas

### Critical Assumptions to Test

#### Desirability Assumptions
1. **Families willing to trust AI with household management**
   - Risk: High | Evidence: Low

2. **Users will adopt voice/chat interface for home tasks**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: Medium (Alexa/Google adoption exists)

3. **Mental load reduction is worth $10-20/month**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: Low

4. **Users will input enough data for AI to be useful**
   - Risk: High | Evidence: Low

#### Feasibility Assumptions
5. **AI can learn household routines effectively**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: Medium

6. **Smart home integrations can be maintained reliably**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: High (APIs exist)

7. **Privacy concerns can be adequately addressed**
   - Risk: High | Evidence: Medium

#### Viability Assumptions
8. **Customer acquisition cost under $50**
   - Risk: Medium | Evidence: Low

9. **Churn rate below 20% monthly**
   - Risk: High | Evidence: Low

### Proposed Experiments

#### Experiment 1: Problem Validation Survey
- **Tests:** Assumptions #1, #3
- **Method:** Survey 100 target users about household management pain points
- **Success Metric:** 60%+ report significant mental load, 40%+ interested at $15/month
- **Time/Cost:** 1 week, $100 (survey incentives)

#### Experiment 2: Concierge Service
- **Tests:** Assumptions #1, #4, #5
- **Method:** Manually play "Rong" for 5 families via messaging for 2 weeks
- **Success Metric:** Families engage 3+ times daily, report 30%+ reduction in stress
- **Time/Cost:** 2 weeks, $0 (time only)

#### Experiment 3: Voice Interface Prototype
- **Tests:** Assumptions #2, #4
- **Method:** Build simple voice bot for task/reminder management, test with 10 users
- **Success Metric:** 70%+ prefer voice over manual input, use 5+ times/week
- **Time/Cost:** 3 weeks, $500

#### Experiment 4: Landing Page + Pre-orders
- **Tests:** Assumptions #3, #8
- **Method:** Landing page with pricing, collect $10 pre-orders for beta access
- **Success Metric:** 3%+ conversion rate, 50+ pre-orders, CAC under $50
- **Time/Cost:** 2 weeks, $300 (ads)

#### Experiment 5: Privacy & Trust Study
- **Tests:** Assumption #7
- **Method:** Interview 20 users about data concerns, test different privacy models
- **Success Metric:** 70%+ comfortable with local-first/encrypted approach
- **Time/Cost:** 2 weeks, $0
